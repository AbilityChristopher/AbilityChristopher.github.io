Why Menstrual Hygiene Management Is More Than a Health Issue: A Conversation About Education, Dignity, and Opportunity

When discussions about education and youth development take place, attention is often focused on academic performance, school infrastructure, technology, and teacher quality. While these factors are important, one critical issue that directly affects the educational experience of millions of girls remains overlooked: Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM).

Menstrual hygiene is not simply a health matter. It is an education issue, a dignity issue, a gender equality issue, and a community development issue.

Recently, I had the opportunity to participate in a Menstrual Hygiene Management sensitization program for students of LGEA Primary School and Universal Basic Education (UBE) Kanshio, Makurdi, Benue State. The experience reinforced an important lesson: empowering young people requires addressing the practical challenges that affect their daily lives.

Understanding Menstrual Hygiene Management

Menstrual Hygiene Management refers to the access, knowledge, and resources required for girls and women to manage menstruation safely, hygienically, and with dignity.

Effective menstrual hygiene management includes:

  • Access to accurate menstrual health education
  • Availability of sanitary products
  • Safe water and sanitation facilities
  • Supportive school and community environments
  • Freedom from stigma and discrimination

When these elements are absent, the consequences can extend beyond health concerns and directly affect educational outcomes.

The Link Between Menstrual Health and Education

Many students experience anxiety, embarrassment, or discomfort during menstruation due to misinformation, cultural stigma, or lack of resources.

In some communities, girls miss classes because they do not have adequate menstrual hygiene materials or because they fear being stigmatized by peers. Every missed school day can affect confidence, participation, and academic performance.

Creating awareness around menstrual health helps students:

  • Build confidence
  • Improve self-esteem
  • Stay engaged in learning
  • Make informed health decisions
  • Develop healthy personal hygiene habits

For educators, parents, and community leaders, supporting menstrual health is an investment in educational success.

Breaking the Silence Around Menstruation

One of the greatest barriers to menstrual health awareness is silence. For many young people, menstruation remains a topic surrounded by myths, misconceptions, and embarrassment.

Open and age-appropriate conversations help students understand that menstruation is a normal biological process rather than something to fear or hide. Schools and community organizations have an important role to play in creating safe spaces where students can ask questions, receive accurate information, and develop confidence in managing their health.

The Role of Community Outreach Programs

Community outreach initiatives serve as powerful platforms for education and empowerment. Through awareness campaigns, health talks, and student engagement programs, organizations can bridge knowledge gaps and provide practical guidance that positively impacts young lives.

Participating in the Menstrual Hygiene Management sensitization program in Makurdi demonstrated how collaborative efforts between community organizations, educators, and volunteers can contribute to healthier and more informed school environments. These initiatives do more than share information; they help build confidence, encourage healthy behaviors, and promote student wellbeing.

Why Youth Development Requires a Holistic Approach

Youth empowerment extends beyond academic achievement. To develop their full potential, young people need support in multiple areas, including health education, digital literacy, financial literacy, personal development, leadership development, and emotional wellbeing.

When communities invest in these areas, they create environments where young people can thrive both inside and outside the classroom. This holistic approach is essential for sustainable development and long-term social impact.

Moving Forward

Building stronger communities begins with addressing the challenges that affect young people's ability to learn, grow, and succeed. Menstrual Hygiene Management awareness is one of many interventions that contribute to student wellbeing and educational inclusion.

As educators, development practitioners, volunteers, and community advocates, we all have a role to play in ensuring that every young person has access to the knowledge, resources, and support needed to reach their potential. By promoting health education, challenging stigma, and supporting youth-centered initiatives, we move closer to creating a future where every student can learn with confidence, dignity, and opportunity.

Final Thoughts

True development happens when education, health, and empowerment work together. The conversations we have today about menstrual hygiene, student wellbeing, and youth development can create lasting impacts for future generations. Every awareness session, every outreach activity, and every effort to educate young people is an investment in a stronger and more inclusive society.
