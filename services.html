What I Offer

Services

From one-on-one counselling to large-scale community training — here is how I show up for the people I serve.

Education with a purpose. Impact you can feel.

Everything I do flows from one conviction: every person carries untapped potential — and the right guidance, at the right time, unlocks it.

My services are built around the three roles that define me — Educator, Counsellor, and Public Speaker — and are available to individuals, schools, NGOs, community organisations, and corporate groups.

I bring field experience, professional training, and a genuine passion for human development to every engagement — whether it's a quiet one-on-one session or a stage full of hundreds.

Guidance & Counselling

Core Service

Rooted in my B.Ed in Guidance and Counselling, this service provides structured, confidential, and empathetic support for individuals navigating life decisions, career crossroads, and personal development challenges.

Whether you are a student figuring out your next step, a young adult feeling stuck, or an organisation looking to support its people — I offer a safe space to think, process, and move forward with clarity.

  • One-on-one personal and career counselling sessions
  • Career assessment and educational path guidance
  • Goal-setting and accountability mentoring
  • Group counselling workshops for schools and organisations
  • NYSC and post-graduation transition support
Best for: Secondary school students, university graduates, NYSC corps members, young professionals, and organisations seeking employee wellbeing support.

Digital Literacy Training

Community & Institutional

As a certified facilitator under the NITDA DL4ALL (Digital Literacy For All) initiative, I deliver practical digital skills training to individuals and community groups — with a focus on those in the informal sector who have had little or no digital exposure.

My approach is hands-on, jargon-free, and context-sensitive — making digital tools accessible and relevant to everyday life in Nigeria.

  • Basic ICT skills: smartphone use, email, internet navigation
  • Mobile banking and digital financial tools
  • Online safety, data privacy, and scam awareness
  • Social media for personal branding and small business
  • Digital tools for NGOs, community groups, and educators
Best for: Market traders, community groups, informal sector workers, rural women's organisations, and institutions running digital inclusion programmes.

💰

Financial Literacy Education

Schools & Youth Orgs

As a graduate of the Financial Literacy Leadership Fellowship (KAFI), I equip students and young adults with practical money skills — the kind schools rarely teach but life demands immediately.

My financial literacy sessions are interactive, relatable, and rooted in the Nigerian economic reality — covering everything from basic budgeting to understanding financial systems.

  • Budgeting and saving habits for students and young adults
  • Understanding income, expenses, and financial planning
  • Introduction to entrepreneurship and income generation
  • Avoiding debt traps and financial scams
  • Long-term thinking: goals, investment basics, and wealth mindset
Best for: Secondary and tertiary schools, NYSC programmes, youth empowerment NGOs, and community financial literacy drives.

🏢

HRM Training & Workforce Development

Organisational

With multiple certifications in Human Resource Management, I support organisations and teams in building healthier, more productive work cultures — with a focus on people, not just processes.

This service is particularly relevant for NGOs, community organisations, and small businesses in Nigeria looking to formalise and strengthen how they manage their people.

  • Workforce onboarding and orientation frameworks
  • Team communication and conflict resolution workshops
  • Staff motivation and performance mindset sessions
  • Volunteer and community worker management
  • HR fundamentals training for team leads and supervisors
Best for: NGOs, community organisations, small businesses, and team leads looking to improve people management and workplace culture.

Youth Mentorship

Individual & Group

Beyond formal counselling, I offer ongoing mentorship to young people who need a consistent, trusted voice in their corner — someone who will challenge them, cheer them on, and help them stay on course.

This is less structured than counselling and more relationship-based — built around the young person's unique journey, goals, and challenges.

  • Regular one-on-one check-ins and accountability sessions
  • Scholarship and application guidance
  • Leadership and character development conversations
  • Group mentorship circles for youth organisations
  • Life skills: discipline, focus, communication, resilience
Best for: Secondary school leavers, university students, young professionals (18–30), and youth-focused organisations seeking mentorship programming.
