Rising Above Your Circumstances

📅 2026 ⏱ 4 min read ✍️ Ability Christopher

Where you come from is not where you have to stay. That sentence sounds simple — almost like a motivational poster. But the truth behind it is harder and more important than any slogan. Because rising above your circumstances is not about ignoring where you came from. It is about refusing to be defined by it.

The Weight of Context

In Nigeria, the circumstances of your birth carry enormous weight. Which state you are from. Whether your parents were educated. Whether you had electricity growing up, access to good schools, or a family that could afford to send you to university. These things matter. They shape your starting point in ways that are very real and very unfair.

I am not going to pretend otherwise. I am not going to tell you that hard work alone is enough, or that if you just believe, everything will work out. The systemic inequalities in our society are real. They are not excuses — they are facts.

But here is what I have also seen to be true: circumstances are a starting point, not a sentence.

Your background may explain where you began. It does not have to determine where you end.

The People Who Proved It

Every community in Nigeria has stories of people who defied the odds. The first in their family to attend university. The young woman from a poor background who built a business from nothing. The boy from a rural village who became a doctor, a lawyer, a leader. These are not fairy tales. They are real people who made real decisions in real, difficult circumstances.

What did they have in common? Not luck — though some had it. Not unusual talent — though many were gifted. What they shared was a refusal to accept that their circumstances were the final word on their lives. They found one open door and walked through it. Then they found another. Then another. Progress rarely comes all at once. It comes door by door.

What Rising Above Actually Requires

Honesty about where you are. Not self-pity, but clear-eyed awareness of your starting point and the real challenges you face. You cannot navigate well if you are pretending the terrain is different from what it is.

Community. The people around you matter enormously. Finding even one person — a mentor, a teacher, a cousin who has gone further — who believes in your potential and will speak into your life can change everything. You were not designed to rise alone.

Patience with the process. Rising above circumstances is rarely a single dramatic moment. It is usually a long series of unglamorous choices, made consistently, over time. The results come — but they come slowly, and they come to those who keep showing up.

Faith. In God, in yourself, in the possibility that your story is not finished yet. Faith is not passive — it is the fuel that keeps you moving when the evidence around you says to stop.

To Anyone Who Feels Stuck

If you are reading this and your circumstances feel overwhelming — I want you to know that I see you. The weight you are carrying is real. The obstacles in front of you are real. Your frustration is completely valid.

But I also want you to know this: the fact that you are still here, still reading, still looking for something — that is not nothing. That is a seed. And seeds, given the right conditions, become things that nobody expected.

Your circumstances are not your destiny. They are your starting material. And what you build with them — that is your story to write. Nobody else can write it for you. And nobody else can stop you from writing it either.

Rise. One step at a time. But rise.
