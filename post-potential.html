God Gave You Potential — Now What?

📅 2026 ⏱ 4 min read ✍️ Ability Christopher

There is a phrase I have heard all my life in churches, classrooms, and motivational speeches across Nigeria: "You have so much potential." It is meant to be encouraging. And it is. But somewhere along the way, I started to wonder — potential for what, exactly? And more importantly, what am I actually doing with it?

Because here is the thing nobody tells you: potential is not an achievement. It is a starting point. And a starting point means nothing if you never move.

Potential Is Not an Achievement

A seed has the potential to become a tree. But a seed buried in bad soil, never watered, never given light — remains a seed forever. It never becomes what it was designed to be. The same is true for us. We can be told our whole lives that we are gifted, intelligent, talented, called — and still end up living far below what we were created for, simply because we never moved from potential to action.

The graveyard is the richest place on earth — full of books that were never written, businesses that were never started, songs that were never sung, and lives that were never fully lived.

That is a sobering thought. And it should be. Because every one of us carries something that, if left unactivated, will die with us. The question is not whether you have potential. The question is what you are choosing to do with it.

The Accountability That Comes With Gifting

I believe deeply that God gives every person gifts and capacity. But I also believe that the possession of those gifts comes with a responsibility — a stewardship. You are not just a recipient. You are a custodian. And custodians are accountable for what they do with what they have been given.

That accountability is not a burden. It is actually a gift in itself. It means your life is not accidental. It means the things you care about, the skills you carry, the experiences you have been through — they are not random. They are raw material. And what you build with that raw material is your contribution to the world.

What Activation Actually Looks Like

Activating your potential does not always look dramatic. It rarely starts on a big stage or in front of a large crowd. It usually starts quietly — with a decision to be consistent, to serve where you are, to keep showing up even when no one is watching.

For me, it has looked like showing up to facilitate a digital literacy session for traders who had never used a smartphone. It has looked like sitting with a confused secondary school leaver and helping them see a path forward. It has looked like writing this article at the end of a long day, because I believe the words matter even when the audience is small.

None of that is glamorous. All of it is purposeful. And purpose, lived out consistently in small things, is how you build something that lasts.

So — Now What?

If you are reading this and you know — deep down — that you are living below what you are capable of, I want to ask you directly: what is one thing you can do today to begin closing that gap?

Not tomorrow. Not when conditions are perfect. Not when you feel more confident or more ready or more equipped. Today. With what you have. Where you are.

God gave you potential. The question was never whether you have it. The question is what you are going to do with it. And only you can answer that.
