In‑depth looks at initiatives I’ve led or contributed to

Ser-Av Foundation – Strategy Refinement

Role: Lead facilitator & strategic analyst

Challenge: The organisation faced inefficiencies in three key areas: operations, institutional partnerships, and human resources.

Key Gaps Identified

  • Operations: Communication breakdowns and high financial costs
  • Institutions: Lack of reliable, long‑term partnerships
  • Human Resources: Shortage of personnel and expertise

Solution Approach

Led a two‑day strategy workshop using gap analysis and root‑cause mapping. Developed actionable recommendations for each area.

Impact

The team adopted new communication protocols, partnership criteria, and a phased recruitment plan. Early results showed a 30% reduction in operational friction.

NITDA DL4ALL – Digital Literacy for the Informal Sector

Role: Certified Digital Champion & Field Facilitator

National Goal: Contributing to Nigeria's ambitious target of achieving 70% digital literacy by 2027 as part of the Digital Literacy for All (DL4ALL) initiative, a presidential mandate.

Target Audience & Outreach

Taking digital literacy directly to where people live and work. As part of a nationwide team, I've helped train over 500+ individuals, including:

  • Informal Sector Workers: Market women, traders, and artisans in local markets
  • Rural Communities: Farmers, pedestrians, and residents in underserved areas
  • Young Learners: School children and out-of-school youth
  • Vulnerable Groups: Senior citizens and others at risk of digital exclusion

Training Delivery

  • Conducted one-on-one and group training sessions in marketplaces, community hubs, and public spaces
  • Taught foundational modules: device operation, internet navigation, online safety, and digital communication
  • Adapted training to different literacy levels and learning paces
  • Provided post-training support to reinforce digital confidence

Impact

Participants gained practical skills such as using mobile banking, accessing government services online, and identifying online fraud risks. Many reported increased confidence in using digital tools for daily transactions and business activities.

Being part of a movement where digital champions are reaching citizens in motor parks, religious places, and village squares—ensuring no one is left behind in Nigeria's digital transformation.

KAFI Foundation – Kafipedia Pan-Africa Financial Literacy Competition

Role: Facilitator & Financial Literacy Champion

Objective: Equip secondary school students with practical financial knowledge – budgeting, saving, investing, and financial discipline – through an interactive competition format.

My Contribution

  • Delivered financial literacy training sessions to students preparing for the competition
  • Taught core concepts: goal‑based saving, avoiding debt traps, and smart spending habits
  • Mentored participants on how financial literacy connects to career and life goals
  • Collaborated with KAFI team to design engaging learning materials

Outcome & Impact

Students demonstrated improved understanding of budgeting and savings. Several participants reported applying the lessons to manage their pocket money and set savings goals. The competition environment boosted confidence and interest in financial independence.

“Now I know why I should save before spending.” – Student participant
